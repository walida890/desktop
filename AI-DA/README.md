# Meridian Financial — AI Data Analyst Capstone Programme

**5 Tasks · 10 Weeks · Real DA Portfolio**

---

## What Is This Programme?

You are a Junior Data Analyst working at **Meridian Financial**, a UK fintech that provides personal loans, mortgages, and insurance. The FCA (Financial Conduct Authority) has issued a 90-day regulatory notice: Meridian must prove it is handling customer data correctly and reporting accurately under Consumer Duty rules.

Your job as the Junior DA is to audit the current data landscape, identify gaps, propose solutions, plan testing, and help prepare the regulatory submission. You will do all of this while using AI tools to research, draft, and analyse — but you must always review and challenge the AI output before submitting anything.

This is a portfolio programme. At the end of 10 weeks, you will have a professional body of work that demonstrates real DA skills: stakeholder analysis, data auditing, requirements, solution design, testing, and executive communication.

---

## The Company

**Meridian Financial** — UK fintech, 3 years old, ~50 employees.
- Provides personal loans, mortgages, and insurance products
- Has grown fast but data governance has not kept pace
- FCA has given 90 days to demonstrate Consumer Duty compliance

**Your stakeholders:**
- Sarah Chen — Head of Compliance (your main contact)
- James Okafor — Chief Data Officer
- Priya Sharma — Head of Engineering
- Tom Whitfield — CEO

---

## How the 10 Weeks Work

Each task runs for 2 weeks. You will receive a brief, do BA/DA work, and submit deliverables. AI is your working tool throughout.

**Weekly rhythm:**
- Week 1: Read brief → conduct analysis using the notebooks and AI prompts
- Week 2: Draft deliverables → review AI output → submit

---

## The 5 Tasks

### Task 1 — Discovery & AS-IS Analysis (Weeks 1-2)
**Brief:** Sarah Chen asks you to map Meridian's current data landscape and identify where Consumer Duty gaps exist.

**What you do:**
- Create a stakeholder register
- Map the AS-IS data flows
- Identify gaps against FCA Consumer Duty requirements
- Write a discovery report

**Deliverable:** Discovery Report with stakeholder map, process map, and gap register

---

### Task 2 — Requirements Engineering (Weeks 3-4)
**Brief:** James Okafor needs a requirements package that the engineering team can build from.

**What you do:**
- Write functional and non-functional requirements
- Prioritise the backlog (Must / Should / Could)
- Map requirements to the gaps found in Task 1
- Run a requirements review session (using AI to draft review notes)

**Deliverable:** Requirements Package with prioritised backlog and review evidence

---

### Task 3 — Solution Design & Integration (Weeks 5-6)
**Brief:** Priya Sharma needs to understand what the engineering team should build and how it fits together.

**What you do:**
- Map requirements to proposed solutions
- Identify integration points and gaps
- Evaluate 3 vendor/approach options
- Produce a solution architecture summary and gap closure plan

**Deliverable:** Solution Design doc with options analysis and data model gaps

---

### Task 4 — UAT Planning & Test Scenarios (Weeks 7-8)
**Brief:** Sarah Chen needs assurance that the solution will actually work before submission.

**What you do:**
- Write a UAT strategy
- Create test scenario register (positive and negative paths)
- Map test scenarios back to requirements (traceability matrix)
- Identify what data you need to run the tests

**Deliverable:** UAT Plan with test scenarios and traceability matrix

---

### Task 5 — Executive Presentation & Regulatory Submission (Weeks 9-10)
**Brief:** Tom Whitfield needs a 10-slide deck and draft FCA submission to review before the deadline.

**What you do:**
- Write an executive summary
- Build a 10-slide presentation
- Draft the FCA submission letter
- Prepare Q&A responses

**Deliverable:** 10-slide deck + executive summary + draft FCA submission

---

## How to Use the Notebooks

Each task has a Jupyter notebook (.ipynb) in the `notebooks/` folder. Open it in Jupyter, VS Code, or Google Colab.

**Each notebook contains:**
1. **Context cells** — explain what you need to do and why
2. **TODO cells** — sections you fill in with your own analysis
3. **AI Prompt cells** — copy these into ChatGPT, Claude, or Copilot to accelerate your work
4. **Visualisation cells** — run these to generate charts, diagrams, and tables
5. **Export cells** — save your outputs to CSV for use in the next task

**Important:** AI generates the first draft. You review it, challenge it, and refine it. You must understand every word in your final submission.

---

## AI Guidelines

### Use AI for:
- Researching FCA Consumer Duty guidance
- Drafting structure and language for documents
- Generating options analysis frameworks
- Summarising long regulatory documents
- Creating first-draft tables and lists

### NEVER trust AI for:
- FCA regulatory citations (it hallucinates these constantly)
- Specific Meridian data or facts (it does not know this company)
- Prioritisation decisions (these require judgement)
- Test scenarios for complex edge cases (it misses failure paths)

### Every submission must include:
- A note of what AI helped you produce
- What you changed and why
- Any claims you verified yourself
