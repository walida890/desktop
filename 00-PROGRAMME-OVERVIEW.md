# AI-Augmented Business Analyst Capstone Programme
## "Meridian Financial: The 90-Day Compliance Sprint"

---

## Programme Structure

| Task | Focus | Key BA Skills | AI Integration Depth |
|------|-------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Task 1** | Discovery & AS-IS Analysis | Stakeholder mapping, process mapping, regulatory gap analysis | AI as BA co-pilot throughout |
| **Task 2** | Requirements Engineering | User stories, FR/NFR, acceptance criteria, prioritization | AI drafts → mentee critiques/refines |
| **Task 3** | Solution Design & Gap Analysis | Technical feasibility, system integration mapping, data model review | AI cross-references requirements vs. existing systems |
| **Task 4** | UAT & Test Scenario Design | Test strategy, scenario writing, traceability matrix | AI generates test cases → mentee challenges coverage |
| **Task 5** | Stakeholder Presentation & Recommendations | Executive communication, risk reporting, go-live planning | AI scaffolds deck → mentee drives narrative |

---

## Mentee Profile
- Someone transitioning into BA, or a junior BA with 0–12 months experience
- No prior AI tool experience required — the programme teaches AI as a working practice, not a technical skill
- Access to: ChatGPT (or equivalent), a process mapping tool ( Miro / Lucidchart / draw.io), a requirements tool (Notion / Confluence / Google Docs)

## The Regulatory Context (Real FCA Requirements)

The mentee works against real FCA obligations. Not make-believe rules — real regulatory frameworks:

- **FCA PS17/14** — Financial Conduct Authority guidance on anti-money laundering systems and controls
- **FCA MLR 2017** — Money Laundering Regulations: customer due diligence requirements
- **5AMLD / 6AMLD** — EU Anti-Money Laundering Directives (applicable post-Brexit as retained law)
- **JMLSG Guidance** — Joint Money Laundering Steering Group guidance (widely used in UK industry practice)
- **FCA Consumer Duty** — FG22/5 (newer): fair treatment of new customers in onboarding

The mentee does NOT need to be a compliance expert — but they must demonstrate they understand the *business consequence* of getting this wrong and can translate regulatory intent into clear requirements.

## AI Tooling Philosophy

AI is the **working environment** for this programme, not a feature.

At each task, the mentee receives:
1. A **prompt template library** — proven AI prompts for that BA activity
2. A **critical review checklist** — questions to ask of AI output before trusting it
3. A **red lines** document — where AI consistently goes wrong in BA contexts

The mentor session for each task focuses heavily on **AI output review** — is the AI generating plausible-but-wrong requirements? Is it inventing regulatory references? Is it defaulting to generic language instead of specific, testable criteria?

This is the actual skill: **knowing when to trust AI and when to override it.**
